How Technology Changed Gaming

Video gaming began in the 1970s with simple systems like Atari. Over decades, advancements in hardware transformed gaming from basic pixels to immersive experiences. It's amazing how far we've come with games today.

The 1980s introduced home consoles like NES and Sega Genesis. These systems brought 2D classics like Super Mario Bros. and Sonic into homes, making gaming a global phenomenon.

The 1990s saw the rise of 3D graphics with consoles like PlayStation and Nintendo 64. Gamers explored vast worlds and experienced deeper storytelling, setting new standards for the industry.

Today, consoles like PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X offer cutting-edge tech, including VR and 4K graphics. These innovations enable developers to create expansive, cinematic experiences.

Advanced hardware has enhanced every aspect of gaming, from AI to sound design. The result? Richer, more immersive games that captivate players worldwide.
